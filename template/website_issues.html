Small clickable areas for links in the site.

Hyperlinks are designed to be clicked, so to make them usable, it makes sense to ensure that they are easy to click. Here is an example of links that are far too small; clicking them is harder than it should be.

Here is an example of the same interface element, the comments link, but this time with a much larger clickable area:

Why would we want a larger clickable area? Simple. Because our hand movement with the mouse is not very precise. A large clickable area makes it easier to hover the mouse cursor over the link. To ensure we get a large clickable area, we could either make the whole link bigger or increase the padding around the link using the CSS padding property. Here is the code:

.menulink { margin-right: 5px; color: white; background-color: #4CAF50; padding: 12px; font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; cursor: pointer; display: inline; text-decoration: none; color: white }

Duplicate Page Titles

The title of each Web page is important. Page titles are the pieces of text we write between the title tags in the head section of our HTML code. Sometimes people create a generic title while working on their website s template, their website s name, for example : and then re-use the same title across the whole website. This is wrong because it robs each page of a couple of key benefits.

The first benefit is that a good title communicates to your visitors a lot of information about what the page is about. People can quickly figure out if they are in the right place or not. Remember that this title does not just show at the top of the browser window; it is also shown on the search engine results pages. When people see a list of results on a search engine like Google, they read the page title to figure out what each page is about. This alone is a good enough reason to spend a little time optimizing your page titles.
